The Problem
Farmers Are Left Behind
When Crops Fail

Small farmers in India face financial instability from crop losses due to unpredictable weather, pests, and disasters — with little recourse.

The Core Problem

Indian small farmers face financial instability from crop losses, with limited access to insurance and slow, complex claim processes that delay urgently needed compensation.

The Impact

Farmers fall into debt and income loss, households struggle financially, rural economies weaken, and insurers face low participation and inefficient claims — a systemic failure.

Root Causes

Limited awareness among farmers about available crop insurance schemes and their eligibility

Complex procedures and documentation requirements make filing claims inaccessible

Inadequate access to risk-management mechanisms in rural areas

Unpredictable climate, pests, and natural disasters causing frequent, severe crop failures

Informal loans from moneylenders increase debt cycles when farmers seek alternatives

Our Solution
Automatic. Transparent.
Farmer-First.

A weather-triggered system that automatically releases insurance payouts to farmers when extreme weather events cause crop damage.

🌦️

Weather-Triggered Payouts

When extreme weather is detected via real-time APIs and satellite data, insurance compensation is automatically released — no manual claim filing needed.

📱

Simple Mobile App

Farmer registration, policy tracking, claim status, and payout notifications all in one app. Multilingual support and minimal documentation required.

🔍

Transparent Tracking

Real-time claim status updates, transparent payout progress, and SMS/WhatsApp notifications keep farmers informed at every step.

🤝

No Middlemen

Verified weather data eliminates the need for intermediaries, reducing delays, paperwork, and dependency on agents for small and marginal farmers.

🌐

Multilingual Support

Built for rural India with local dialect support, simple language, and an interface designed for low digital literacy users.

📊

Satellite Intelligence

Satellite imagery combined with weather APIs ensures objective, data-driven crop loss assessment that is fair and hard to dispute.
